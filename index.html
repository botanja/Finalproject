Political Prisoners in Russia

The level of repression in Russia is "unprecedented" since the Stalinist era. According to OVD-Info, an independent human rights defence and media group, 1,011 people in Russia are held in prison for political reasons. Among them are:

Politicians

  • Alexei Navalny

    The Russian opposition leader and anti-corruption activist Alexei Navalny is recognized as a prisoner of conscience by Amnesty International and was awarded the Sakharov prize for his "immense bravery". Learn more about Alexei Navalny

  • Ilya Yashin

    The Russian opposition politician Ilya Yashin was sentenced to 8 years and 6 months imprisonment for "spreading false information", meaning telling the truth, about the killings in Bucha. Learn more about Ilya Yashin

  • Vladimir Kara-Murza

    The Russian and British political activist and journalist Vladimir Kara-Murza was sentenced to 25 years in prison for allegedly spreading false information about the Russian military. Learn more about Vladimir Kara-Murza

Artists

  • Aleksandra Skochilenko

    The Russian artist, musician and poet Aleksandra Skochilenko was sentenced to 7 years in prison for replacing grocery store price tags with anti-war messages. Learn more about Aleksandra Skochilenko

  • Evgenia Berkovich

    Evgenia Berkovich is a Russian theatre director and screenwriter and poet. Since May 2023 she has been in pre-trial detention for allegedly justifying terrorism and faces 7 years in prison. Learn more about Evgenia Berkovich
